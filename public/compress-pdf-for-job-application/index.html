Why compress a PDF before applying for jobs?

  • Portal upload limits — Many ATS systems (Workday, Taleo, Greenhouse) cap uploads at 2–5MB. A large PDF can fail to upload.
  • Email delivery — Some corporate email servers block attachments over 10MB. Staying under 1MB ensures reliable delivery.
  • Faster loading — Recruiters may preview your CV online; a smaller file loads faster and makes a better impression.
  • WhatsApp / Telegram sharing — Referrals often happen over chat. A 200KB PDF sends instantly vs a 5MB file taking minutes.

Does compression affect my CV text quality?

No. Our compressor only re-encodes embedded images (like a headshot or company logo in your CV). All text, fonts, and vector graphics are completely untouched and remain perfectly sharp at any zoom level. Recruiters and ATS systems can still search, copy, and parse text from the compressed PDF.

Best compression level for a resume PDF

  • Medium — Recommended for most CVs. Reduces file size by ~80% while keeping quality high.
  • High — Use only if you have a photo-heavy CV and need to get under 500KB.
  • Low — Near-original quality; minimal size reduction. Not usually needed for job applications.

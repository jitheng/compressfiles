Email attachment size limits by provider

  • Gmail — 25MB per email, but large attachments convert to Google Drive links
  • Outlook / Hotmail — 20MB per email; corporate Outlook may be as low as 10MB
  • Yahoo Mail — 25MB per email
  • Apple Mail (iCloud) — 20MB; larger files use Mail Drop
  • Corporate email servers — Often 10MB or less; some legacy servers cap at 2–5MB

Even within the technical limits, smaller attachments load faster for the recipient — especially on mobile. Keeping PDFs under 1MB is a best practice for professional communication.

Which compression level should I use for emails?

  • Medium — Best for most email use cases. Keeps document text razor-sharp while reducing file size by ~80%. Ideal for reports, invoices, contracts, and CVs.
  • High — Best when you need to meet a strict size limit (e.g., under 500KB). Some image quality is sacrificed but text remains fully readable.
  • Low — Near-original quality with modest reduction. Use when the recipient will print the document and quality matters most.

Why does PDF file size matter?

Most email providers cap attachments at 10–25MB, but many job portals, university portals, and government forms require files under 1MB or even 500KB. A large PDF can also slow down document sharing over WhatsApp or Slack.

Our compressor re-encodes embedded images at a lower JPEG quality without altering text, so the document remains fully readable at any zoom level.

What compression level should I use?

  • High — Best for achieving under 1MB. Reduces images to ~35% quality. Ideal for web uploads, archiving, and portals.
  • Medium — Good balance for email and sharing. Usually reduces to 1–3MB range.
  • Low — Near-original quality. Best for print or when quality is critical.

Frequently asked questions

Will the PDF still be readable after compression?

Yes. Text is never re-encoded and stays perfectly sharp at any zoom level. Only embedded images are re-compressed at a lower JPEG quality.

Is my file kept private?

Absolutely. Files are processed in memory and deleted from the server immediately after download. Nothing is stored.

What if my PDF is still over 1MB after High compression?

If the PDF contains very high-resolution images or is already lightly compressed, try splitting it into separate pages before compressing each section individually.
